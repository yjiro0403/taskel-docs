## Morning: Planning (Step 3 + Step 4)

**What to do:** Arrange today's tasks by time blocks and add time estimates

1. Open **Daily View**.
2. Check incomplete tasks from yesterday (Chuted) and tasks to do today.
3. Distribute tasks into **Morning / Afternoon / Evening** sections.
4. Add **time estimates** to each task.
5. Check the **estimated end time**. If it shows "11:40 PM," it won't fit. Move something to tomorrow.

**AI Tip**: Ask the AI "Show me today's tasks" for a quick status overview. "What are my goals this week?" helps confirm priorities.

## Evening: Reflection (Step 6 + Step 7)

**What to do:** Chute if unfinished, reflect on estimate vs. actual gaps

1. Check if all tasks are completed.
2. For **incomplete tasks**, use Chute to flow them to tomorrow, or decide to drop them. **Don't blame yourself.**
3. Click the **Daily Note** icon and jot down today's insights.
4. Review the **estimate vs. actual** gap. "The 30-minute estimate actually took 50 minutes" â€” this awareness sharpens your time sense.

## Start of Week: Breakdown (Step 2)

**What to do:** Break monthly goals â†’ this week's goals â†’ today's actions

1. Open **Weekly View** to check and set this week's goals.
2. Working backward from monthly goals, write specific things to do this week.
3. Decide whether to carry over last week's incomplete tasks.
4. Add tasks connected to this week's goals into today's Daily View.

## Start of Month/Year: Goal Setting & Revision (Step 1 + Step 8)

**What to do:** Set annual goals / Revise goals based on insights

### New Year / Getting Started (Step 1)
1. Set annual goals in **Yearly View**.
2. They don't need to be perfect. **Goals are living things** â€” they're expected to change.
3. Break annual goals into monthly goals using **Monthly View**.

### Every Day / Week / Month (Step 8)
1. Based on what you noticed in reflection (Step 7), revise your goals.
2. "This goal might not fit me anymore." "I realized this other thing matters more." â€” **Welcome** such changes.
3. Record goal changes and their reasons in **Weekly Notes** or **Monthly Notes**.

> **ðŸ’¡ Key point:** Step 8 is the core of the Taskel Method. Changing doesn't mean failing â€” **changing means growing**.
